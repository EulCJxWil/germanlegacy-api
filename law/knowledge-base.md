# German Citizenship Knowledge Base — US Applicants
## Uwe Kindschuh / germanlegacy.net
Compiled from ~500 sent emails + staplehill FAQ. US applicants only.
Last updated: 2026-03-20.

---

## 1. Dual Citizenship / Obligations

You do not have to give up your US citizenship, learn German, pay German taxes (unless you move to Germany), or have any other obligations.

- No application to the US State Department is needed to keep US citizenship.
- German taxes apply only to tax residents of Germany or earners of German-sourced income — not to citizenship.
- German citizenship once acquired is permanent. You do not lose it by staying in the US, keeping your US passport, or doing nothing.
- Criminal conviction can prevent naturalization: https://www.reddit.com/r/GermanCitizenship/comments/14ve5tb/

## 2. Document Rules — General

**What requires certified copies:**
- Birth certificates, marriage certificates, death records, naturalization records, German documents: certified copies OR originals required.
- Passports and driver's licenses: regular uncertified copies are fine.
- FBI background check: regular printed PDF is fine (no certification needed).

Exact phrasing: "passports and FBI background checks can be sent as a regular copy. Everything else must be certified copies."

**Apostille:**
Never needed for US documents. "No apostille is needed." / "Nothing needs to be apostilled."
Exception: applicants from non-Hague-Convention countries (e.g. Bolivia) need apostilles.

**Translation:**
Documents in English do not have to be translated into German.

**One document per family:**
"Each document is needed only once even though you will submit [X] applications."

**Originals:**
You can send original documents, but you will not get them back. Most applicants prefer certified copies. For consulate submission: bring originals, the consulate makes certified copies, you keep the originals.

---

## 3. How to Get Certified Copies

Options:
1. **German consulate/embassy**: Bring originals to any appointment. Staff makes certified copies; you keep the originals.
2. **Notary public** (in states that allow it, e.g. Washington State): A notary can make "notarized copies" — equivalent to certified copies for German purposes.
3. **California**: California does not allow notaries public to certify copies. German missions are the only option.
4. **Order a new official copy** from the issuing authority (e.g. vitalchek.com for birth/marriage certificates) — the newly issued document IS the certified copy.
5. **USCIS naturalization records**: USCIS Genealogy Program cannot provide certified copies. Both the Washington DC German Consulate and the Italian Consulate have confirmed: submit the photocopy of the naturalization certificate together with the USCIS Genealogy Program response letter and mailing envelope.

You do NOT need to get certified copies before your consulate appointment. Bring your originals to the appointment and the consulate certifies them there.

## 4. FBI Background Check

Required for: §5 (StAG 5 / EER naturalization by declaration).
NOT required for: Art. 116 GG, §15 (StAG 15), Feststellung.

**How to apply (electronic, preferred):**
URL: https://www.edo.cjis.gov/#/do-info
Timing: result within 24 hours to a few days (less than a week).
Result: FBI emails a PDF. Print the PDF and submit it. No certification needed.

**What the PDF looks like:**
Two pages, FBI logo as watermark. Page 1: name and address. Page 2: FBI logo, "U.S. Department of Justice" letterhead, date, name, file number, result ("no prior arrest data"), FBI agent signature.

**Validity:** Cannot be older than 6 months at the time of submission.

**Minors:** FBI cannot fingerprint minors. Submit without the background check. Worst case, BVA will request one by the time they process the application (in 2+ years), at which point the minor will likely no longer be a minor.

## 5. Consulate — Appointments

**Only ONE person needs to appear.** That person can bring all signed applications and original documents for the entire family group. "Only one appointment is needed to submit all applications. The other applicants do not have to appear at the consulate."

**Booking categories by consulate** (citizenship submission):

| Chicago | 1283 | 986 |
| New York | 1937 | 2360 |

*Washington DC categoryId 1473 says it "cannot be used for citizenship matters" — this means you cannot SUBMIT an application during that appointment, but you CAN get certified copies of documents, which can then be mailed or given to whoever is submitting.

The citizenship submission appointment also covers copy certification. No separate appointment needed.

Chicago accepts out-of-jurisdiction applicants.

## 6. Consulate — What Happens at the Appointment

No interview. The consulate's job is to collect documents, make copies, and forward the package to BVA.

Typical flow:
- Arrive, announce name at doorbell, go through security.
- Leave phone and laptop in mailbox outside (no electronics inside).
- Wait, then go to window.
- Staff collects the Erklärung/application forms.
- Staff goes to back office to make copies of all documents (~20 minutes).
- Entire appointment: ~60-90 minutes.
- You get all original documents back.

Note: Consulate staff are NOT specialists in citizenship law. They may be unfamiliar with specific pathways (StAG 5, EER). BVA is the authoritative decision-maker; the consulate is only a document collection point. Uwe's advice: lead with a one-sentence description of the pathway, and offer the cover letter early.

For complicated cases: "They are more likely to just say 'Feststellung' if you send them an email with complicated facts and more likely to go through the documents if you show up in person." — Skip any pre-appointment email and just show up.

## 7. Mailing Directly to BVA (without consulate)

Both options are valid — consulate or direct mail.

Address:
```
Bundesverwaltungsamt
Barbarastrasse 1
50735 Köln
Germany
```
(Use street address, not PO box — FedEx cannot deliver to PO box.)

Do not use a tracking method that requires the recipient to sign.
From outside the EU: customs declaration, goods = "documents", value = 1 USD/EUR.

Problem: Many US states do not allow notaries to make certified copies. In those states, applicants must visit a German consulate to get certified copies anyway. Many therefore choose the consulate submission route for this reason.

## 8. Application Signatures

The applications can be signed at home. The signatures do not have to be notarized. The signatures must be original (not scanned, printed, or e-signed). You can change the date next to each signature to the actual date of signing.

Where to sign:
- **Adult StAG 5/EER applicants**: last page of main application form, "Unterschrift der erklärenden Person"
- **Adult Feststellung/§116 applicants**: page 5, "Unterschrift der antragstellenden Person"
- **Minor's application (all types)**: last page; father signs left ("Unterschrift der ersten gesetzlichen Vertretung"), mother signs right ("Unterschrift der zweiten gesetzlichen Vertretung")

If only one parent has legal custody: only that parent signs.

## 9. After Submitting — Timeline

1. **2–4 months**: BVA sends file number (Aktenzeichen) letter by mail.
   - Sample letter: https://i.imgur.com/DQOh9yN.jpeg
   - English translation: https://i.imgur.com/YeeaeIm.jpeg
2. **Do NOT email BVA to ask about status.** BVA will contact you if they need anything. Standard BVA response to status inquiries: "All applications/declarations are processed in the order of receipt. Due to an increased volume, there are currently longer processing times. Please understand that we cannot provide information about interim statuses or the anticipated duration of the overall process. Should we have any questions or require additional documents, we will contact you proactively."
3. **If anything changes** (address, name, marital status): email BVA at staatsangehoerigkeit@bva.bund.de (write in English; they reply in German).
4. **BVA will not deny an application for missing documents** — they will specify what additional documents are needed and request them.
5. **Application approved**: BVA sends Einbürgerungsurkunde (certificate of naturalization).
   - For StAG 5/EER cases: the naturalization date is backdated to ~6 months after submission.

## 10. German Passport After Approval

Must appear IN PERSON at a German consulate. Fingerprints required. Cannot be done by mail.

Bring:
- 2 passport photos (EU format, 35×45mm)
- Passport application form (Uwe prepares these for clients)
- Citizenship certificate / supporting documents
- Birth certificate listing city or town of birth (county alone is not sufficient)

Options and fees:
- Regular passport: 106 EUR
- Extra pages (48-page passport): +31 EUR
- Express: +32 EUR
- Identity card: 70.60 EUR

Processing time: approximately 6–8 weeks.

## 11. Keeping German Citizenship for Future Generations

For applicants born since 2000: "Your possible future children will get German citizenship only if their birth is registered with a German mission before their first birthday."

For applicants born before 2000: "Your children will get German citizenship at birth automatically, but your grandchildren only if they are registered with a German mission before their first birthday."

Registration URL: https://www.germany.info/us-en/service/03-citizenship/german-citizenship-acquired-through-notification-of-birth-occuring-abroad-943378

## 12. Name Discrepancies on Documents

BVA looks at ALL consistent information across all documents, not just the inconsistent name. If other identifiers (date of birth, place of birth, parents' names) consistently identify the same person, the name discrepancy is not a problem.

Common cases:
- **Middle name variation** (Louise vs. Lou): Not a problem if other identifiers match.
- **Maiden vs. married name** without a marriage certificate: Only a problem if the linking documents have no other consistent identifiers (birth date, place, parents).
- **Name on birth certificate vs. ID**: Usually not a problem if they are consistent — no additional records needed.

Application forms:
- "Current last name" = name as shown on current passport under "Surname"
- "Last name at birth" = name on birth certificate (or written in ALL CAPS on older documents)
- "Given names" = names on current passport under "Given names"
- Application forms do NOT ask for given names at birth.

## 13. Missing or Destroyed Documents

BVA will not deny an application for missing documents. They will ask for what they need.

Strategy: Submit everything you have and let BVA ask for more.

Fallbacks for missing German documents:
- Population register (Meldekarte/Melderegister) — often survives when civil records were destroyed. Uwe helps request these from German town halls/archives.
- Baptism certificate from a church.
- Ship arrival records (certified copies from NARA: https://www.archives.gov/research/immigration/passenger-arrival.html).
- BVA accepts a birth record from Germany before 1914 as sufficient proof of German citizenship for that ancestor.

Application forms — "unknown" or approximate values are acceptable:
- Unknown naturalization date: write "around 1955", "in the 1950s", or "unknown".
- Unknown information about non-German-line parent: write "approximately" or "unknown".
- Ancestor's residence history: BVA has never asked for more detail even when very broad (e.g. "lived from birth to death in Germany"). No examples of BVA challenging this.
- End date of citizenship for deceased ancestor: no end date needed. Including the death date as end date is also fine.

## 14. Minors — Signatures and Consent

For citizenship applications: both parents sign the last page of the minor's application (left = father, right = mother). The other parent does NOT need to appear at the consulate.

For passport applications (after citizenship is approved):
- Children 16 or older: must also sign their own passport application.
- Children under 16: do not sign. Parents sign.

Custody documents: Copies with an original court stamp are sufficient (do not need to be certified copies).

---

## 15. Parents Not Married

No marriage certificate needed or expected when parents were not married.

**German citizenship through mother (out of wedlock)**: birth certificate showing mother's name is sufficient.

**German citizenship through father (out of wedlock)**: birth certificate must show father's name, plus proof of paternity acknowledgment (Vaterschaftsanerkennung form). Request a certified copy of this form from the office where the birth was registered. (US hospitals forward the original paternity acknowledgment form to the responsible American registry office at the time of birth.)

Divorce records between parents are NOT needed if the name on the birth certificate matches the name on the ID.

---

## 16. US Birth Certificates

Order new ones at vitalchek.com. The newly issued certificate is the certified copy.

Key requirement: the birth certificate must show the parents' names.

For passport applications (separate from citizenship application): the birth certificate must list the city or town of birth — county alone is not sufficient.

---

## 17. Family Members in Different Consulate Jurisdictions

Options:
1. One person gets certified copies at their local consulate, mails them to the family member who submits the whole package.
2. Each person submits at their own local German mission.
3. Mail originals to the submitting person; they get them certified at their appointment; mail them back.

---

## 18. Adoption

A legal adoption severs the biological descent line. You can claim German citizenship based on your adopted parent's ancestors, not your biological parent's ancestors.

If the adoption resulted in a new birth certificate showing the adoptive parent, use that certificate.

---

## 19. Born in Germany

Germany does not grant citizenship by birthplace (no jus soli for adults). German citizenship comes only from descent.

Being born in Germany does not mean you already have German citizenship or can skip the application. Exception: a birth record from Germany before 1914 is accepted by BVA as sufficient proof of citizenship for that ancestor.

---

## 20. Adding Family Members / Submitting Incomplete Package

You can bring the signed applications and documents you have and send missing documents later by mail (include the file number once you have it).

To add a new family member not included in the original submission: submit a new separate application. If submitted before the file number arrives, identify the application by name and date of birth. Once file number is known, include it on the cover letter.

Post-approval: if the naturalization certificate date is backdated to before a child's birth, the child gets German citizenship automatically at birth — no separate application needed. If the date is after the child's birth, an additional application is needed later.

## 21. Vollmacht (Power of Attorney)

A Vollmacht is NOT part of a standard citizenship application. Each applicant signs their own application at home. No Vollmacht is needed for one family member to submit applications for others at the consulate — the submitting person simply brings all signed applications and documents.

**When a Vollmacht IS required:**

1. **Adult children (16+) who turned 16 after the application was originally submitted.** BVA requires a statement from them that (a) they consent to receiving German citizenship and (b) they grant power of attorney to the parent acting on their behalf. Uwe prepares these statements (as .docx files). The child signs it; no notarization or certification of the signature is required. A copy of the child's current driver's license or passport must accompany the statement (copy does not need to be notarized or certified).

   Exact phrasing: "a statement from your children that they consent to receiving German citizenship, which is needed since they turned 16 since the application was submitted, and that they give you power of attorney. I prepared these statements for you (see attached) which have to be signed by the respective child"

2. **Requesting privacy-protected German records from a Standesamt (German vital records office).** German birth and marriage records are under privacy protection for 110 years. When a Standesamt requires a Vollmacht, they typically ask for: a power of attorney issued by the person entitled to request the record (or proof of direct descent), ID copies of both the requester and the person granting the Vollmacht, and a copy of the birth certificate proving descent. Uwe handles this as part of his service — he prepares the request and the client sends it to the German office.

**When a Vollmacht is NOT required:**
- To submit applications at the consulate on behalf of other family members (just bring their signed applications)
- For signatures on citizenship applications (each applicant signs their own)

## 22. Direct-to-Passport (for Those Who Already Have German Citizenship)

Applies when both parents were German citizens at the time of birth — no citizenship application needed. Apply directly for a German passport at a German consulate.
